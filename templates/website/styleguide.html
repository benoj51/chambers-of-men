The organic "cm" script. Curves echo the contours of rock and ascent. Not to be confused with the retired boxed "CM" initials.

Mark only

Used in nav, favicons, and compact contexts. Minimum 24px tall.

Lockup

Used in heroes, footers, and deck title slides. Minimum 60px tall.

Logos delivered 2026-04-19 as PNG files (BLACK / WHITE, mark + lockup). Wired into the system via CSS mask-image — the mark takes the parent element's color, so it can be rendered in any brand token without additional assets. 03b — Serif review

Which serif matches the curvature of the mark?

The serif system landed on Newsreader (Option D) after a four-way comparison. The logo's curvature is more pronounced than any Garamond can carry — the mark has zero stroke contrast and bulbous, soft terminals. Newsreader's lower contrast, softer terminals, and flowing italic match the mark while keeping the editorial register. Kept here as a record of the decision.

06 — Don’ts

Anti-patterns

Check every template against this list. These are the drift points — the things that silently pull the brand back toward v1 or toward generic digital.

No teal or gold. The v1 tokens are retired. Terracotta replaces teal. Sage replaces gold.

No boxed “CM”. The bordered-square nav placeholder is retired. Use the organic script or the awaiting-SVG slot.

No pure white. Cream (#EFE9DB) is the reading colour. Bright white feels generic and breaks the mood.

No Material Symbols. Feather icons, 1.5px stroke. Material is The Flock’s system, not this one.

No missing grain. Every page needs the body::after noise overlay at opacity .018.

No stacked accents. Terracotta and sage next to each other without cream or dark between them will vibrate.

No emoji or filler. Voice is warm and considered. Never “we’re excited” or “just wanted to check in”.

No showy motion. Reveal on scroll, soft hovers, orb floats. No parallax, no spring bounces.
